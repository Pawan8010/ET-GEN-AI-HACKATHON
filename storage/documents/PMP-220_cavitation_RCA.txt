PMP-220 Cooling-Water Pump Cavitation Root Cause Analysis
Document status: DEMONSTRATION DATA for hackathon validation
RCA reference: RCA-2026-04-PMP220.

Pump PMP-220 experienced high broadband vibration, gravel-like noise, and fluctuating discharge pressure during afternoon peak demand. Inspection found the suction strainer 62 percent blocked by cooling-tower debris. The reduced suction pressure caused cavitation; bearing damage was not the initiating cause.

The first why was unstable flow due to cavitation. The second why was inadequate net positive suction head. The third why was excessive suction-strainer differential pressure. The fourth why was debris accumulation. The fifth why was failure to execute the weekly strainer inspection after the maintenance route was reassigned.

Corrective actions: clean the strainer, restore the weekly inspection route, alarm differential pressure at 0.35 bar, verify suction pressure before starting the standby pump, and review cooling-tower screen integrity. Vibration returned below 3.2 mm/s after cleaning.
